# Research-001: Great Filter & Civilization Survival Hypothesis

## Status
Research (Hypothesis)

## Core Question
Why do civilizations with extraordinary technological power still fail to become long-term sustainable civilizations?

## Background
Humanity has demonstrated increasing technological capability, yet continues to face existential risks including war, ecological stress, pandemics, AI risks and governance challenges.

This suggests technology alone is insufficient for civilization advancement.

## Working Hypothesis
Civilization progress depends on the co-evolution of:

- Technology
- Governance
- Collective Wisdom
- Long-term Cooperation

If technology advances significantly faster than the other dimensions, existential risk increases.

## The Great Filter
Possible filters include:

1. Resource exhaustion
2. Self-destructive warfare
3. Misaligned artificial intelligence
4. Ecological collapse
5. Institutional failure
6. Cultural fragmentation

## CivilOS Research Goal
CivilOS is not intended to predict the future.

Instead, it provides experimental environments where different civilization strategies can be simulated and compared.

- Can collective wisdom emerge through repeated simulation?
