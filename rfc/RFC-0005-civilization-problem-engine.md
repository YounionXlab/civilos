# RFC-0005: Civilization Problem Engine v0.1

## Purpose

Civilizations are not driven by quests.

They evolve because new problems continuously emerge.

The Problem Engine is responsible for generating, connecting and evolving civilization-scale challenges.

---

# 1. First Principle

Problem is the fundamental unit of civilization evolution.

Environment changes → Problems emerge → Decisions are made → Civilization evolves.

---

# 2. Problem Lifecycle

Environment Change
↓
Problem Emergence
↓
Awareness
↓
Decision
↓
Action
↓
Resolution / Escalation
↓
Historical Memory
↓
New Problems

Problems never truly disappear.
They transform into new forms.

---

# 3. Problem Categories

Natural:
- Climate
- Disease
- Disaster
- Resource depletion

Social:
- Governance
- Inequality
- Trust
- Conflict

Technological:
- AI alignment
- Energy
- Biotechnology
- Cybersecurity

Civilizational:
- Planetary expansion
- Cultural divergence
- Long-term survival
- Great Filter risks

---

# 4. Problem Network

Problems are connected rather than isolated.

Example:

Energy shortage
→ Food production declines
→ Public dissatisfaction
→ Political instability
→ Migration
→ Institutional reform

Each solution may create new problems.

---

# 5. Game Design

Traditional games:
Quest → Completion

CivilOS:
Problems continuously emerge.
Players, AI agents and institutions cooperate or compete to solve them.

---

# 6. Design Philosophy

A civilization should not become stronger simply by accumulating technology.

It becomes more mature by improving its ability to solve increasingly complex interconnected problems.
